Our Kids

Infant

Babies are individuals and this is an egocentric stage of life. Babies cannot and should not be asked to understand that their immediate needs cannot be met. They do not all nap at the same time, eat in the same manner, or reach developmental milestones as a group. Teaching partners plan accordingly for their group, taking into account the individual stages and needs of each child in the group. Infants thrive and grow when surrounded by other children, however, we continue to respect their individual needs. We know we cannot be “Parents” but we think that this model of care is the next best thing.

Toddler

When your child is all over the place, they're discovering their world. Toddlers learn best in safe, loving places where they can explore, in their own way. The activities for this age group are built around toddlers' limitless curiosity and their natural desire to push boundaries.

Preschool

Preschool is an educational option for parents in Canada that is not mandatory and is for children who are too young to attend junior-kindergarten or kindergarten school programs. These programs, run by school boards across Canada in most provinces and territories, usually start at age four or five, depending on the province you live in.
